## üéØ Brand Overview

### **Brand Name**
**ONE YEAR**

### **Abbreviation**
**1Y**

### **Tagline (Primary)**
"What does your year really buy?"

### **Alternative Taglines**
- "Track purchasing power and wealth concentration across generations"
- "Stop thinking in dollars. Start thinking in years."
- "See what one year of work really buys"

### **Brand Mission**
Help people understand and discuss economic changes across generations by introducing a new unit of measurement that makes purchasing power visceral and personal.

### **Brand Promise**
We cut through economic jargon and provide clear, defensibly accurate data that gives people the language to discuss what they've always felt but couldn't articulate.

### **Target Audience**

**Primary:**
- Millennials and Gen Z (ages 25-45)
- Feeling economic squeeze but lack data to explain it
- Social media users who share infographics
- Politically engaged but frustrated with partisan talking points

**Secondary:**
- Educators teaching economics or financial literacy
- Journalists covering economic inequality
- Policy advocates seeking shareable data
- Parents explaining economic changes to their kids

- **Digital:** 24px diameter minimum
- **Print:** 0.25 inches diameter minimum
- Below these sizes, text becomes illegible

## ‚úçÔ∏è Typography

### **Typeface Family: Inter**
**Primary:** Inter (Google Fonts, free)  
**Fallback:** Helvetica Neue, Arial, sans-serif  
**Rationale:** Modern, highly legible, excellent for data, free and widely available

### **Type Hierarchy**

**H1 - Hero Headlines**
- **Typeface:** Inter Black
- **Size:** 48-72px (desktop), 32-48px (mobile)
- **Weight:** 900
- **Line Height:** 1.1
- **Letter Spacing:** -2%
- **Color:** Deep Navy (#1a3a52)
- **Usage:** Landing page hero, major section headers
- **Example:** "Stop thinking in dollars. Start thinking in years."

**H2 - Section Headers**
- **Typeface:** Inter Bold
- **Size:** 32-40px (desktop), 24-32px (mobile)
- **Weight:** 700
- **Line Height:** 1.2
- **Letter Spacing:** -1%
- **Color:** Deep Navy (#1a3a52)
- **Usage:** Main section divisions, Act transitions
- **Example:** "What One Year Bought in 1950"

**H3 - Subsection Headers**
- **Typeface:** Inter SemiBold
- **Size:** 24-28px (desktop), 20-24px (mobile)
- **Weight:** 600
- **Line Height:** 1.3
- **Letter Spacing:** 0%
- **Color:** Deep Navy (#1a3a52)
- **Usage:** Category labels, chart titles
- **Example:** "Housing Costs"

**H4 - Labels & Small Headers**
- **Typeface:** Inter Medium
- **Size:** 18-20px
- **Weight:** 500
- **Line Height:** 1.4
- **Letter Spacing:** 0%
- **Color:** Deep Navy (#1a3a52) or Slate Gray (#546e7a)
- **Usage:** Data labels, small section headers
- **Example:** "Median Home Price"

**Body Text**
- **Typeface:** Inter Regular
- **Size:** 16-18px (never below 16px)
- **Weight:** 400
- **Line Height:** 1.6
- **Letter Spacing:** 0%
- **Color:** Deep Navy (#1a3a52) or Slate Gray (#546e7a)
- **Max Width:** 680px for readability
- **Usage:** Paragraphs, explanations, descriptions

**Small Text / Captions**
- **Typeface:** Inter Regular
- **Size:** 14-15px
- **Weight:** 400
- **Line Height:** 1.5
- **Letter Spacing:** 0%
- **Color:** Slate Gray (#546e7a)
- **Usage:** Image captions, footnotes, disclaimers, source citations

**Data / Numbers**
- **Typeface:** Inter Medium (with tabular figures enabled)
- **Size:** Varies by context
- **Weight:** 500-600
- **Features:** Tabular numerals (monospaced digits)
- **Color:** Deep Navy (#1a3a52)
- **Usage:** All numerical data, years, fractions, percentages
- **Why:** Ensures proper alignment in tables and lists

**Emphasis / Callouts**
- **Typeface:** Inter SemiBold
- **Size:** Match surrounding text
- **Weight:** 600
- **Color:** Deep Navy or Burnt Orange (for strong emphasis)
- **Usage:** Key statistics within paragraphs, important quotes

**Links**
- **Typeface:** Inter Medium
- **Size:** Match surrounding text
- **Weight:** 500
- **Color:** Deep Navy (#1a3a52)
- **Hover:** Burnt Orange (#e67e22)
- **Style:** Underline on hover only
- **Usage:** All hyperlinks

---

### **Typography Don'ts**

‚ùå Never use fonts smaller than 16px for body text  
‚ùå Never use pure black (#000000) for text (too harsh)  
‚ùå Never set line length over 680px (hurts readability)  
‚ùå Never use decorative or script fonts  
‚ùå Never use all caps for more than a few words  
‚ùå Never use more than 3 font weights in a single view

## üó£Ô∏è Voice & Tone

### **Core Voice Attributes**

**Educational, Not Condescending**
- We're here to help people understand, not lecture them
- Assume intelligence, explain complexity
- "Let's look at the numbers" not "As you clearly don't know..."

**Conversational, Not Casual**
- Approachable but not flippant
- Professional but not stiff
- "Your grandpa bought a house" not "A male individual in 1950..."

**Data-Driven, Not Cold**
- Facts grounded in emotion
- Statistics connected to real life
- "This is why you can't afford what your parents could"

**Empowering, Not Depressing**
- Gives people language to discuss economics
- Validates their experience
- Provides clarity, not just despair

**Urgent, Not Alarmist**
- Wake-up call without hysteria
- "The data shows..." not "EVERYTHING IS COLLAPSING!"

---

### **Tone by Context**

**Act 1: Empathetic Guide**
- "Let's figure this out together"
- "You're not imagining it"
- "Here's what changed"
- Warm, patient, understanding

**Act 2: Direct Truth-Teller**
- "Here's what the data shows"
- "This is where it went"
- No sugar-coating, but not inflammatory
- Sharp, clear, factual

**Social Media: Accessible Educator**
- Punchy, shareable
- Hook ‚Üí insight ‚Üí link
- "Your grandpa bought a house with 3 months of work. You need 6 years."
- Engaging but substantive

**Educational Materials: Patient Authority**
- Thorough explanations
- Anticipates questions
- Provides sources
- Builds understanding step-by-step

**Error Messages / Help Text: Helpful Friend**
- "Something went wrong" not "ERROR 404"
- "Let's try that again" not "Invalid input"
- Always offer a solution

---

### **Voice Examples**

**‚úÖ GOOD:**
> "In 1960, one year of median wages bought you 1/4 of a house. Today, you need 6 years. Your grandparents weren't better with money. The math just changed."

**‚ùå BAD:**
> "Millenials are broke because housing prices increased while wages stagnated, which is what happens under late-stage capitalism when workers are exploited by the wealthy elite."

**Why?** 
- Good: Personal, clear, data-driven, empowering
- Bad: Partisan, jargon-heavy, preachy, divisive

---

**‚úÖ GOOD:**
> "Elon Musk's wealth equals 3.3 million years of median worker wages. That's 3.3 million people's entire working lives."

**‚ùå BAD:**
> "Elon Musk is hoarding obscene wealth while workers starve! This billionaire scum should be taxed into oblivion!"

**Why?**
- Good: Factual, visceral comparison, lets data speak
- Bad: Inflammatory, emotional manipulation, partisan

---

**‚úÖ GOOD:**
> "The economy grew. Productivity tripled. So where did all that value go? Let's follow the money."

**‚ùå BAD:**
> "The rich stole your wages and now they're laughing at you from their yachts while you can't afford rent."

**Why?**
- Good: Curious, invites exploration, prompts thought
- Bad: Class warfare rhetoric, unproductive rage

## üì± Social Media Guidelines

### **Platform-Specific Approaches**

**Twitter / X**
- **Character Limit:** Get to the point fast
- **Format:** Stat ‚Üí insight ‚Üí link
- **Hashtags:** 1-2 max (#OneYear, #1Y)
- **Example:** "Your grandpa: House = 3 months work. You: House = 6+ years. See what changed: [link] #OneYear"

- **Hook (0-3 sec):** Shocking stat or question
- **Reveal (3-15 sec):** Slide through timeline or comparison

- **Overlay:** Circle badge logo in corner
- **Voiceover:** Conversational, not overly produced

- **Angle:** Professional/career focus
- **Format:** Thoughtful post with context
- **Example:** "Understanding wage stagnation isn't about politics‚Äîit's about math. We built One Year to show purchasing power changes across generations. Check it out for your next economic discussion."

- **Copy:** Personal hook ‚Üí stat ‚Üí link

## üö´ What NOT to Do

### **Messaging Don'ts**

‚ùå **Never:**
- Use "eat the rich" rhetoric or class warfare language
- Make it overtly partisan (no "thanks Obama" or "late-stage capitalism")
- Oversimplify complex economics into good guys vs. bad guys
- Claim to have all the answers or solutions
- Present opinion as fact
- Shame people for their economic circumstances
- Use sarcasm about serious economic hardship

‚ùå **Avoid:**
- Excessive emojis (1-2 max per post)
- All caps (except for brand name "ONE YEAR")
- Clickbait ("You won't BELIEVE what happened in 1975!")
- Fear-mongering ("The economy is COLLAPSING!")
- Conspiracy theories
- Economic jargon without explanation

---

### **Visual Don'ts**

‚ùå **Never:**
- Use the logo on busy backgrounds without a container
- Stretch or distort the circle badge
- Add effects (gradients, drop shadows, 3D)
- Use colors outside the approved palette
- Set body text below 16px
- Use decorative fonts
- Overcrowd layouts
- Use stock photos of "sad millennials with avocado toast"

---

### **Tone Don'ts**

‚ùå **Never sound:**
- Condescending ("Obviously, you don't understand economics...")
- Preachy ("What you NEED to understand is...")
- Dismissive ("Stop complaining and just work harder")
- Hopeless ("Everything is terrible and there's no point")
- Conspiratorial ("THEY don't want you to know...")

## üìä Brand Personality

If ONE YEAR were a person:

**They would be:**
- A patient teacher who's genuinely excited about data
- Your smart friend who can explain economics without jargon
- Someone who validates your frustration with facts
- Curious, not cynical
- Angry about injustice but not performatively outraged

**They would NOT be:**
- A politician with an agenda
- An influencer chasing engagement
- A doomer preaching apocalypse
- A corporate spokesperson spinning stats
- A condescending academic

**Their superpower:**
Making complex economics feel personal and understandable

**Their weakness:**
Sometimes so focused on data accuracy that emotional resonance takes a backseat (we're working on balancing both)
