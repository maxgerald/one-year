# ONE YEAR - Project Outline

## üéØ Project Vision

A financial literacy tool that helps people understand and discuss economic changes across generations by introducing a new unit of measurement: **1Y (One Year)** = one median worker's annual salary.

By normalizing everything to "years of work" instead of dollars, we bypass inflation confusion and make economic reality visceral and personal.

---

## üé≠ Two-Act Structure

### **Act 1: The Personal Hook** (Primary Experience)
*"Why can't I afford what my parents could?"*

**Purpose:** Build trust and personal connection through relatable purchasing power comparisons
**Tone:** Educational, empathetic, "let's figure this out together"
**Focus:** Individual worker's purchasing power across time

**Key Message:** "Your grandparents weren't better with money. The math was just different."

### **Act 2: The Context** (Secondary Discovery)
*"Oh... THAT'S where it went"*

**Purpose:** Contextualize personal economic squeeze within systemic wealth concentration
**Tone:** Factual but sharp, data-driven revelation
**Focus:** Wealth inequality and concentration over same time period

**Key Message:** "Worker productivity tripled. Wages stayed flat. The economy grew. Where did it go?"

---

## üìè Core Innovation: The 1Y (One Year) Unit

### **Definition:**
- **1Y** = One median worker's annual salary for that year
- Always normalized to contemporary prices
- Represents actual TIME of worker's life

### **Why This Works:**
- ‚úÖ Removes inflation confusion
- ‚úÖ Makes time/labor the fundamental currency
- ‚úÖ Creates intuitive fractions (1/3 car vs "$18,000")
- ‚úÖ Builds emotional weight (fractions = months of your life)
- ‚úÖ Bypasses partisan talking points
- ‚úÖ Impossible to argue with - it's pure math

- Clean circle with "1Y" in center

- Suggests official standard/unit of measurement

## üéØ Key Messaging & Tone

### **Act 1 Messaging:**
‚úÖ **Do:**
- "Let's look at the numbers together"
- "This helps explain what you're feeling"
- "Your grandparents weren't smarter with money"
- "The math changed"

‚ùå **Don't:**
- Blame individuals
- Use partisan language
- Sound preachy or condescending
- Overwhelming with too many numbers at once

### **Act 2 Messaging:**
‚úÖ **Do:**
- "The data shows..."
- "Here's where it went"
- Present facts, let users draw conclusions
- Include policy context without blame

‚ùå **Don't:**
- "Eat the rich" rhetoric
- Conspiracy theories
- Oversimplify complex economics
- Make it purely about outrage

### **Overall Tone:**
- **Educational first**, outrage second
- **Data-driven** but emotionally resonant
- **Empowering** - gives people language to discuss this
- **Defensible** - every claim sourced

## üé® Design Principles

### **Visual Hierarchy:**
1. **Primary:** The fraction bars (core innovation)
2. **Secondary:** Year slider (exploration)
3. **Tertiary:** Specific numbers (detail)

### **Color System:**

**Primary Color:**
- **Deep Navy Blue** (#1a3a52)
  - Usage: Main branding, logo, headers, primary UI elements
  - Represents: Trust, authority, depth (like deep water/space)
  - Works on: White and light backgrounds
  - Emotional tone: Professional, serious, credible

**Accent Color:**
- **Burnt Orange** (#e67e22)
  - Usage: CTAs, key data highlights, urgency markers
  - Represents: Wake-up call, attention, energy
  - Use sparingly: Only for emphasis
  - Emotional tone: Alert without alarm

**Supporting Colors:**
- **Slate Gray** (#546e7a) 
  - Usage: Secondary text, borders, subtle UI elements
  - Neutral, readable, professional
  
- **Off-White** (#f5f5f5)
  - Usage: Backgrounds, cards, breathing room
  - Softer than pure white, easier on eyes

**Act-Specific Colors:**

**Act 1 (Personal/Warm):**
- Primary: Deep Navy (#1a3a52)
- Accent: Warm Green (#7cb342) for positive data
- Alert: Muted Orange (#ff9800) for concerning trends
- Background: Off-White (#f5f5f5)

**Act 2 (Systemic/Sharp):**
- Primary: Deep Navy (continuity from Act 1)
- Alert: Deep Red (#d32f2f) for extreme wealth gaps
- Contrast: Dark Slate (#37474f) for dramatic data
- Keep navy for consistency

**Data Visualization:**
- Positive trends: Warm green (#7cb342)
- Negative trends: Burnt orange (#e67e22)
- Critical/extreme: Deep red (#d32f2f)
- Neutral: Slate gray (#546e7a)

### **Typography:**
- **Headers:** Bold sans-serif (Inter Bold or Helvetica Neue Bold)
  - Size: 24-48px for hero, 18-24px for sections
  - Weight: 700-900
  
- **Body:** Readable sans-serif (Inter Regular or Roboto)
  - Size: 16-18px minimum (accessibility)
  - Line height: 1.5-1.6 for readability
  - Weight: 400-500

- **Data/Numbers:** Tabular figures, monospace
  - Use when displaying: Years, fractions, percentages
  - Ensures alignment in tables/lists

- **Accent/Emphasis:** Inter SemiBold or Medium
  - For key stats, callouts, labels

### **Accessibility:**
- WCAG AA compliance minimum (AAA preferred)
- Color-blind friendly palettes (tested with simulators)
- Sufficient contrast ratios (4.5:1 minimum for text)
- Screen reader optimized HTML semantics
- Keyboard navigation for all interactive elements
- Alt text for all images and data visualizations
