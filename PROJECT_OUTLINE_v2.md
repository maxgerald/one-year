## 🎯 Project Vision

A financial literacy tool that helps people understand and discuss economic changes across generations by introducing a new unit of measurement: **1Y (One Year)** = one median worker's annual salary.

By normalizing everything to "years of work" instead of dollars, we bypass inflation confusion and make economic reality visceral and personal.

---

## 🎭 Two-Act Structure

### **Act 1: The Personal Hook** (Primary Experience)
*"Why can't I afford what my parents could?"*

**Purpose:** Build trust and personal connection through relatable purchasing power comparisons
**Tone:** Educational, empathetic, "let's figure this out together"
**Focus:** Individual worker's purchasing power across time

**Key Message:** "Your grandparents weren't better with money. The math was just different."

### **Act 2: The Context** (Secondary Discovery)
*"Oh... THAT'S where it went"*

**Purpose:** Contextualize personal economic squeeze within systemic wealth concentration
**Tone:** Factual but sharp, data-driven revelation
**Focus:** Wealth inequality and concentration over same time period

**Key Message:** "Worker productivity tripled. Wages stayed flat. The economy grew. Where did it go?"

## 🔑 Core Innovation: The 1Y (One Year) Unit

### **Definition:**
- **1Y** = One median worker's annual salary for that year
- Always normalized to contemporary prices
- Represents actual TIME of worker's life

### **Why Median Income?**
We use **median** (not mean/average) because:
- Mean income is inflated 37% by wealthy outliers
- Median represents what the **typical** worker actually earns
- 50% earn more, 50% earn less - it's the true middle
- Government standard (Census Bureau, BLS use median for this reason)
- Impossible for billionaires to skew the calculation

### **Current 1Y Values (December 2025):**

| Metric | Value | Source |
|--------|-------|--------|
| **Median Individual FT Earnings** | $63,128/year | BLS Q3 2025 |
| **Median Household Income** | $83,730/year | Census 2024 |
| **Federal Minimum Wage (FT)** | $15,080/year | $7.25/hr × 2080 |

**Primary metric:** Median Individual Full-Time Earnings ($63,128)
**Why:** Represents "one year of YOUR labor" - not blended with spouse/household

### **Income Selection Options (Planned):**
- [x] Median Income (default) - $63,128
- [ ] Minimum Wage - $15,080 (Phase 2)
- [ ] Household Income toggle - $83,730 (Phase 2)

### **Why This Works:**
- ✅ Removes inflation confusion
- ✅ Makes time/labor the fundamental currency
- ✅ Creates intuitive fractions (1/3 car vs "$18,000")
- ✅ Builds emotional weight (fractions = months of your life)
- ✅ Bypasses partisan talking points
- ✅ Impossible to argue with - it's pure math

## 🎨 App Flow & Features

### **Landing Page**
**Hero Section:**
> **"Stop thinking in dollars. Start thinking in years."**
> 
> One Year (1Y) = one median worker's annual pay.
> In 1975, 1Y bought 1/x of a home.
> In 2025, 1Y buys 1/y of a home.
> 
> Where did those extra years of your life go?

**Primary CTA:** "Make a share card" → Act 1  
**Secondary CTA:** "Explore the timeline"

### **Start Here (Act 1 - Story Spine)**
**3-step flow:** Feel it → Explore it → Share it

1) **Feel it (Hero):** The 1975 vs 2025 home comparison  
2) **Explore it (Timeline):** Slide through time, compare essentials  
3) **Share it (Cards):** Generate a social-ready graphic

### **Act 1: Main Interactive Timeline**

#### **Controls:**
- **Year Slider:** 1913 - 2025 (granular, year-by-year with interpolation)
- **Income Type:** Toggle (Median Income | Minimum Wage) ← Phase 2
- **View Mode:** Toggle between "Years" and "Months"
 - **Baseline Year Selector:** Compare any year vs a chosen baseline
 - **Category Filters:** Show core only or all categories
 - **Story Notes Toggle:** Show narratives tied to specific items

#### **Display Categories:**
Each shows as a fraction bar + months conversion:

**Housing:**
- 🏠 Median home purchase (in fractions of 1Y)
- 🏢 One-bedroom apartment rent (annual, in fractions of 1Y)

**Transportation:**
- 🚗 New car purchase
- ⛽ Annual gas costs

**Food & Essentials:**
- 🍞 Annual grocery basket (USDA moderate plan)
- 🔌 Annual utilities

**Family Costs:**
- 👶 Childcare (per child, if applicable)
- 🎓 State college tuition (4-year total)

**Healthcare:**
- 🏥 Annual health insurance premium
- 💊 Average out-of-pocket costs

**Bottom Line:**
- 💰 Remaining for savings/emergency fund
- 📊 "Total lifestyle cost: X.X of 1Y"

#### **Key Interactive Features:**

**1. "Build Your Life" Calculator**
- User checks boxes for desired lifestyle elements
- App calculates total 1Y cost across different years
- Shows when lifestyle became mathematically impossible

**2. "Your Year" Personal Timeline**
- User enters birth year
- Compares what parents' 1Y bought then vs. what user's 1Y buys now
- Generates shareable card

**3. "The American Dream" Preset**
- Single income household
- 3-bedroom home
- 2 kids through college
- New car every 5 years
- Annual vacation
- Shows: "Affordable from 1950-1978. Requires dual income from 1979-present."

**4. Minimum Wage Toggle (Phase 2)**
- Switch between median income and minimum wage views
- Shows how purchasing power differs dramatically
- Minimum wage 1Y = $15,080 (federal) or state-specific

### **Act 2: Wealth Concentration Context**

#### **Transition Screen:**
> "You just saw how much less your Year buys now. But the economy grew. Productivity tripled. Corporate profits soared.
> 
> So where did all that value go?"

#### **Wealth Timeline Display:**

**Top Section: Worker 1Y (Your Perspective)**
- Visual: Single worker icon = 1Y
- Lifetime: ~40 years of 1Y (working career)
- Median lifetime earnings: ~$2.5M at current wages

**Bottom Section: Billionaire Wealth (in 1Y)**
- Rockefeller (1913): 1.4 million years of 1Y
- Getty (1976): 2.1 million years of 1Y
- Bezos (2025): 3.0 million years of 1Y
- **Musk (2025): 7.6 million years of 1Y**

**Visual Representation:**
- Stack of tiny worker icons reaching to space
- "If each icon = 1 person's entire working life..."
- "Elon Musk's wealth equals 190,000 people working their entire careers"

**Key Charts:**
- Wealth concentration % over time
- CEO-to-worker pay ratio timeline (21:1 in 1965 → 344:1 today)
- Productivity vs. wage growth (divergence since 1979)
- "Where productivity gains went" (pie chart)

- User selects item + two years
- If item has unit data: "1Y buys X gallons of milk"

### **American Dream Deep-Dive**
- Bundle = home + 2 college tuitions + new car + 1 year childcare
- Output: "1Y buys 1/x of the American Dream"
